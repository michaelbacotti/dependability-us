Key Takeaways — April 14, 2026

S&P 500 hits all-time high as Iran ceasefire talks reduce geopolitical risk premium.

VIX 18.36 — elevated but declining; defined-risk spreads remain the preferred structure.

Bull put spreads on SPY at current levels — premium collection in a trending market.

Risk note: VIX 18.36 — Elevated volatility favors defined-risk spreads over naked positions.

Market Commentary

The S&P 500 closed at a fresh all-time high on Tuesday as ceasefire negotiations between Iran and Western powers reduced a key geopolitical risk premium that had been pressuring markets. The relief rally pushed the index above 6,940 for the first time this year, with the VIX dropping to 18.36 as implied volatility compressed.

What Changed Today

Iranian officials confirmed bilateral talks were advancing on nuclear constraints and sanctions relief, which triggered a broad risk-on rotation. Energy equities sold off modestly on reduced supply-shock risk, while technology and consumer discretionary led the advance. The dollar weakened slightly on improved risk appetite.

VIX and Volatility Regime

At 18.36, the VIX remains in elevated territory but has clearly peaked from the mid-20s seen in March. This is consistent with a de-escalation scenario rather than a crisis. In this regime, short premium strategies — iron condors, bull put spreads — work well, but traders should avoid overwriting calls aggressively given the upside momentum.

Preferred Strategy Framework

Current conditions favor defined-risk bullish strategies: bull put spreads to collect premium while betting on higher prices, slightly OTM call spreads for cheap directional exposure, and calendar spreads on SPY if you want to express the view at lower cost. Avoid naked short volatility — the VIX can still spike on headline risk even in a trending rally.

Current Strategy Preferences

📈

Bull Put Spreads

Collect premium while betting on continued upside. Define your risk by buying the lower put. Works well in trending markets with elevated but declining VIX.

Preferred — Elevated VIX
🎯

OTM Call Spreads

Cheap directional exposure to continued upside. Limit loss to the net premium paid. Use 5-10 wide spreads for a high-probability, low-cost way to express a bullish view.

Opportunistic
📅

Calendar Spreads

Sell near-term premium, buy longer-dated option at same strike. Capitalize on time decay differential. Best when VIX is elevated and you expect range-bound price action near-term.
