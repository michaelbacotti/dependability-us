Portfolio Construction Hub

Building a resilient portfolio means understanding how stocks, bonds, and alternatives fit together — and how options can improve the picture.

🗂️

Diversification Basics — Why It Matters

Diversification doesn't guarantee profits — it limits the damage any single event can inflict on your portfolio. A mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, alternatives) that don't move in perfect lockstep reduces overall volatility. The goal is a smoother ride: not maximizing peak returns, but surviving drawdowns well enough to stay invested.

💵

Cash vs. Bonds in a High-Rate Environment

High interest rates change the calculus. Money market funds and short-term Treasuries now yield 4–5%, making them a viable alternative to long-duration bonds. For capital preservation, short-duration instruments reduce interest rate risk while providing competitive yields — a marked shift from the 2010s when bonds dominated.

Drawdown Management

A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover. That's why limiting drawdowns matters more than chasing returns. Pre-defined exit points, trailing stops, and systematic rebalancing help keep your portfolio intact through turbulent markets, preserving capital for the next recovery.

Using Options for Portfolio Protection

Protective puts, collar strategies, and put spreads can hedge your equity exposure without liquidating your positions. Buying index puts as portfolio insurance — particularly when VIX is low and options are cheap — is one of the most cost-effective ways to protect against major drawdowns.

Rebalancing Frequency — When and How

Quarterly rebalancing is common, but the right frequency depends on your risk tolerance and tax situation. Calendar-based rebalancing (quarterly or annually) is simple; threshold-based rebalancing (triggered when an asset drifts 5%+ from target) is more responsive. Over-rebalancing destroys returns through transaction costs and tax events.

📏

Position Sizing Rules

Never allocate more than 1–2% of total portfolio capital to a single defined-risk trade. For uncorrelated positions, 3–5% may be appropriate. Position sizing is the most direct control you have over portfolio risk — it's more important than which strategy you choose.

🧠

Trading Psychology

Emotional decision-making is the primary cause of account destruction. Fear after a loss leads to under-sizing or abandoning strategies that still have an edge. Greed after a win leads to over-sizing and blowing up. A written trade plan and pre-set exits remove emotion from the equation.

⚡ Key Takeaway

A well-constructed portfolio isn't just about returns — it's about surviving downturns well enough to stay invested for the next recovery. Capital preservation and consistent process beat chasing performance every time.
