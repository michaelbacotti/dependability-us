Core Strategy Guides

These are the foundational strategies we use and recommend — all defined-risk, all suitable for self-directed investors.

  • Cash-Secured Puts — Sell puts against stocks you want to own. Collect premium while waiting for a better entry.
  • Covered Calls — Generate income on stocks you already own by selling call options.
  • The Wheel Strategy — Combine cash-secured puts and covered calls into a systematic income cycle.
  • Iron Condors — Profit from low-volatility environments by selling both a put spread and a call spread.
  • Credit Spreads — Put credit spreads vs debit spreads — understanding the difference and when to use each.
  • SPY Weekly Options — Using weekly expiries on SPX/SPY for short-term income and directional trades.

Why Defined-Risk Only?

Every strategy in our education library is defined-risk. That means your maximum possible loss is known before you place the trade — you can never lose more than the margin required to open the position.

We avoid strategies like naked short calls, unhedged short straddles/strangles, and long uncovered puts as lottery tickets.

Defined-risk does not mean risk-free — you can still lose your full premium on a trade. It means you know the worst case going in.
