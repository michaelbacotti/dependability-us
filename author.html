The research team behind Dependability Holdings — who we are, how we work, and why we do this.

Who We Are

Dependability Holdings is an independent research publication. Our research team combines experience in options markets, financial analysis, and investment risk management. We are not a broker-dealer, not a registered investment advisor, and not affiliated with any financial product provider.

We operate as a unified research team rather than a personal finance blog. The work is built around consistent frameworks, repeatable processes, and clear editorial standards — not individual personalities. Our goal is rigorous, reliable market analysis that any informed investor can verify and rely on.

Our Credentials and Frameworks

Our research draws on established, peer-reviewed frameworks that any investor can learn and apply independently:

  • Technical Analysis — Price action, trend identification, support and resistance zones, moving average analysis
  • Options Pricing Theory — Implied vs. realized volatility, probability distribution, Greeks-based risk assessment
  • Macroeconomic Observation — Fed policy direction, GDP trends, employment data, and their effects on equity valuations
  • Risk Management Principles — Position sizing, portfolio construction, and capital preservation frameworks

These are not proprietary systems or secret formulas. They are the same tools used by professional money managers and described in standard finance literature. We present them clearly and apply them consistently.

Who Writes the Forecasts?

The research team produces our daily market outlooks and educational content using our structured six-step methodology:

  1. Collecting publicly available market data (closing prices, VIX, options chains)
  2. Analyzing options flow and volatility structure
  3. Classifying the current VIX regime
  4. Assessing directional and macro signals
  5. Selecting a defined-risk spread strategy suited to conditions
  6. Formatting trade ideas with clear risk/reward parameters

The entire process is documented in our Methodology page. We believe in full transparency about how our analysis is produced.

Who Reviews the Content?

All content produced by the research team is reviewed before publication. The review checks for factual accuracy, consistency with our stated methodology, clarity of presentation, and completeness of risk disclosure. This internal review process is designed to catch errors and maintain the quality standards described in our Editorial Policy.

We do not have outside editors, sponsored contributors, or third-party analysts publishing under our name.

Are You Registered Investment Advisors?

No.

Dependability Holdings LLC is not registered with the SEC or any state securities regulator as an investment advisor. We are an educational publication. We do not manage client money, execute trades on behalf of readers, or provide personalized investment recommendations.

The distinction matters: a registered investment advisor has fiduciary obligations to its clients and is regulated by the SEC or state regulators. We have no such obligations or regulatory status. We produce educational research only.

If you are looking for someone to manage your money or provide personalized investment advice, you should seek a licensed investment advisor or financial planner — not an independent research publication like ours.

Our Independence

We have no financial relationships with any brokerage, exchange, fund manager, or financial product company. We do not receive compensation for mentioning any product or service. We do not accept sponsored content or paid placements.

Our website displays Google AdSense advertisements, which is our only revenue source beyond any holding company operations. This advertising does not influence our editorial content or market commentary in any way.

Our independence is the foundation of our credibility. We have no incentive to recommend any particular product, brokerage, or strategy — only to provide honest, rigorous analysis of market conditions.

Why We Publish

Self-directed investors deserve access to the same analytical rigor that institutional investors apply to their portfolios. Most of what's available online is either oversimplified to the point of uselessness or wrapped in enough jargon to be inaccessible. We try to occupy the middle ground: rigorous enough to be useful, clear enough to be actionable.

We publish because we believe an informed investor is a better investor — regardless of whether they follow our specific recommendations. Our measure of success is reader understanding, not clicks or follower counts.
